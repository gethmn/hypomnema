# Fresh pasta dough

A 00-flour-and-egg dough for hand-cut pappardelle, tagliatelle, or
filled shapes. No yeast; gluten development comes entirely from
kneading.

## Ratios

The classic ratio is one large egg per 100 g of 00 flour, with a
pinch of salt. For richer dough — egg pasta for stuffed shapes — use
extra yolks and a small splash of olive oil.

## Method

Mound the flour on a clean surface, make a well, crack the eggs into
it, and whisk with a fork while drawing in flour from the walls of the
well. Once a shaggy paste forms, switch to your hands and knead 10
minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic.

Wrap and rest 30 minutes at room temperature. The rest is non-optional
— hydrated gluten relaxes during the rest, making the dough easier to
roll thin without snap-back.

Roll through a pasta machine in descending settings, dust lightly with
semolina, and cut to your target shape. Cook in heavily salted water
for 90 seconds.
