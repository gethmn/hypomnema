# Everyday bread

A straightforward white loaf built around commercial yeast. The recipe
trades sourdough complexity for predictable timing — useful when you
want bread on a known schedule.

## Ingredients

- 500 g bread flour
- 10 g instant yeast
- 10 g salt
- 350 g water at 28 °C

## Method

Mix the flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl, then add the water and
stir until no dry pockets remain. Cover and rest 30 minutes; the
autolyse hydrates the gluten before any kneading begins.

Turn the dough out and knead 8 minutes by hand, or 4 minutes on low in
a stand mixer. The dough should pass a windowpane test — stretch a
small piece thin enough to see light through it without tearing.

Bulk-ferment at room temperature for 90 minutes, folding once at the
30-minute mark. Shape into a tight boule, proof seam-down in a floured
banneton for 45 minutes, then bake at 230 °C for 35 minutes on a
preheated stone with steam for the first 10 minutes.
