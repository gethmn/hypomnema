# Olive oil

Pressed juice of olives, valued in cooking for its flavor and its
high content of monounsaturated fats. Quality varies wildly with
cultivar, harvest timing, and processing.

## Extra virgin

The top grade is extra-virgin: oil from the first cold press of
healthy olives, with free acidity below 0.8 percent and no chemical
or thermal extraction. Use it cold or finishing — its low smoke point
(around 190 °C) makes it a poor choice for high-heat searing.

## Storage

Heat, light, and oxygen all degrade olive oil. Keep the bottle in a
cool dark cupboard, decant from a large bottle into a small one if
the original takes more than a few weeks to use up, and treat any oil
older than 18 months as suspect — rancidity adds a sharp, crayon-like
off-flavor that ruins finished dishes.
