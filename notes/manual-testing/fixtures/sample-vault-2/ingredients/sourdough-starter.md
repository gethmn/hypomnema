# Sourdough starter

A live culture of wild yeast and *Lactobacillus* maintained at home
by regular feedings of flour and water. The starter leavens bread,
contributes lactic and acetic acids that flavor the crumb, and acts
as a slow-release reservoir of fermentation activity.

## Establishing a starter

Mix 50 g whole-rye flour with 50 g water in a clear jar. Cover loosely
and rest at room temperature. Discard half and feed with another
50 g flour and 50 g water every 24 hours; after five to seven days
the mixture should triple between feedings — that is the sign the
culture is mature enough to leaven dough.

## Maintenance

Once mature, feed at a 1:5:5 ratio (one part starter, five parts
water, five parts flour) every 12 hours if kept at room temperature
or once a week if refrigerated. Bring a refrigerated starter back to
room temperature and give it two consecutive feedings before using
it to leaven a loaf.
