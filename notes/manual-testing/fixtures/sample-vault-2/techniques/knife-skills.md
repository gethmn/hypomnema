# Knife skills

The biggest cooking-speed win in a home kitchen is consistent dicing
and slicing — uniform pieces cook at uniform rates, which is the
single largest contributor to evenness.

## Grip

Pinch the blade between thumb and index finger just forward of the
bolster; wrap the remaining three fingers around the handle. The
guiding hand on the food curls fingertips inward so the second knuckle
rests against the side of the blade — never the tips.

## Cuts

For dice, square the food into a stable rectangular block first, slice
into planks, stack the planks, slice into batons, and finally rotate
90 degrees and slice across to produce uniform cubes. The planks-then-
batons path produces consistent edges; freehanding straight to dice
produces wedges and slivers.

## Maintenance

Hone the edge before every use; sharpen on a whetstone every few
weeks of regular cooking. A dull knife is the most common cause of
cutting injuries — it slips off food rather than biting in.
